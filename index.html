Framing

Two audiences. One meeting.
Both must leave believing.

Each May meeting contains two distinct audiences who need fundamentally different things from the same 100 minutes. The playbook must address both simultaneously without the meeting feeling split or unfocused. TARA is the red thread that holds the two narratives together.

Investor audience
  • Evaluating the platform's commercial viability and defensibility
  • Needs to see: real engineering, institutional-grade framework, credible team, and a scalable CaaS model
  • Key question: "Is this a sustainable business that outperforms the alternatives available to us?"
  • Concern: AI hype without substance; regulatory risk; team capacity to deliver
  • Success signal: schedules a due diligence session with Dirk + Alex
Family office / client audience
  • Evaluating whether TARA is genuinely useful for their family's most important decisions
  • Needs to feel: understood, not replaced; impressed, not overwhelmed; TARA as companion, not tool
  • Key question: "Will this actually help my family make better decisions at the moments that matter?"
  • Concern: data privacy; AI replacing human judgment; complexity without clarity
  • Success signal: requests a NorthStar session / onboarding conversation
The dual narrative principle Every element of the meeting has been designed to speak to both audiences at once. The SM/Renato framework is the "ticket to the concert" for investors (institutional grade) AND a demonstration of personalised clarity for clients (my portfolio, forward-looking, across four horizons). TARA is architectural defensibility for investors AND companion intelligence for families. The language must bridge both without code-switching visibly.
Which audience is in the room? Before each meeting, Christoph / Dirk must confirm: are we speaking primarily to investor interest, client interest, or both? The phase-by-phase notes below flag where to expand or compress based on the primary audience. The block diagram above shows the universal arc — annotations in each phase show the adjustments.

The problem WH solves — in plain language

Over the lifespan of a multi-generational family, approximately 200 decisions materially shape whether wealth becomes a lasting legacy or dissipates. These decisions are not primarily financial: they are about ownership continuity, succession, governance, capital allocation across time horizons, and values alignment.

Traditional wealth advisors focus on portfolio construction. They give families information and, at best, knowledge. They do not give wisdom. They cannot simultaneously hold the psychological profile of twenty family members, monitor five capital layers in real time, and detect subtle patterns across unstructured conversations. TARA can.

The differentiation — for investors

  • AI + deep investment expertise = the only combination in the market at this quality level
  • Family offices are shifting from short-term trading (AI is winning that) to long-term, horizon-based strategy — exactly where WH plays
  • CaaS architecture: WH is instance one of a repeatable, white-label platform model (LIFTER AI / HELEN)
  • Platform revenue model + advisory relationship = defensible moat

Renato Staub — Guardian Intelligence (Renato leads, 10 min)

Core message: "Traditional risk models are backward-looking and linear. When volatility spikes — exactly when you need your risk model most — they systematically understate your actual exposure. The Guardian Intelligence Engine fixes this."

Three mathematical innovations to cover:

1. Geometric risk aggregation. Risk does not add — it compounds. In high-stress regimes, apparent β = 0.50 becomes realized β = 0.91. Traditional portfolios carry 82% more market risk than they measure at peak stress.

2. Proportional Allocation Rule (PAR). Exposure proportional to the valuation gap: w_active = f × log(V/P). When markets are overvalued: reduce. When undervalued: increase. Mathematical discipline — not emotion. 500 independent 30-year Monte Carlo simulations validate this.

3. Multilayer covariance modeling. 7–8 layers from broad factors to granular assets. Four horizon-specific matrices (one per SM horizon). The same framework that governs the investment allocation governs the structural risk signal TARA surfaces.

Preparation

Objection catalogue

"Why do I need AI for this? My current advisors know my family well."
Your advisors know your family within the constraints of their attention. They see you quarterly. They cannot simultaneously monitor your children's conversations, your liquidity trajectory across four time horizons, and the pattern drift in how you talk about your operating company — and notice when those three things converge into a decision environment forming without you realising it. TARA can. She does not replace your advisors. She makes sure nothing falls between the meetings.
Best answered by: Brian Singer
"What happens to my family's data?"
Your personal data lives in your PersonalSphere — cryptographically isolated from WH's systems and from every other family on the platform. WH advisors see structured analytical summaries that TARA generates — they never see your private conversations or raw data. The architecture enforces this separation at the API level; it is not a policy, it is a technical constraint.
Best answered by: Dirk Boecker / Alex Papratilov
"Is this regulated? What are the compliance implications?"
TARA does not give financial advice — she gives decision intelligence and surfaces structured options for human advisors and family decision makers to act on. The structured brief explicitly directs financial decisions to the WH advisor. The platform is being built with compliance as a hard constraint: data residency, audit logs, consent management, and provenance tracking are all architectural requirements, not afterthoughts. Specific regulatory questions by jurisdiction — we can address those in a technical session.
Best answered by: Christoph Zahneissen / Brian Singer
"This is still prototype-grade. Why are you showing it now?"
Because the families and investors who will shape what this becomes need to be part of defining it — not inheriting a finished product. What you saw today is real: the CoreLens works, the conversation pipeline works, the decision brief is structurally correct. What is prototype-grade is the breadth of scenarios and the polish of the interface. The architecture is solid. We are showing it now because the right anchor relationships are built on shared understanding, not polished brochures.
Best answered by: Brian Singer / Dirk Boecker
"I already have excellent advisors / a family office / a CIO. Where does TARA fit?"
TARA is designed to work alongside your existing advisors — she is not a replacement for them. Contingency

Failure protocol

Technology in front of a high-stakes room always carries risk. The failure protocol defines who owns the recovery and how the meeting continues without TARA if needed.

Decision owner

Dirk Boecker is the demo operator and the failure decision maker. If something breaks, Dirk decides immediately: retry, fallback, or skip. Nobody else intervenes.

Recovery sequences

CoreLens fails (4a)

Switch immediately to preloaded profiles. "Let me show you two profiles we prepared in advance." No explanation needed. The contrast between profiles is the point — it does not require a live survey.

TARA portfolio conversation fails (4b)

Dirk switches to the recorded conversation backup (pre-approved scripted exchange shown as a screen recording, indistinguishable in a meeting setting). Brian narrates: "Let me walk you through the kind of conversation TARA has about a portfolio." Convert to narrative demonstration.

Vineyard prospective brief fails (4c)

Switch to reactive flow script: "Let me show you the reactive version of this — Thomas has just called to say he wants to sell the Vineyard." The brief output is identical; only §1 differs. This fallback is rehearsed and ready.

Total TARA failure (platform down)

Do not apologize extensively. Dirk says: "We have an infrastructure issue — let me use our backup." Switch to a pre-loaded static walkthrough of the Vineyard brief (PDF or slides showing the six-section output). Brian narrates. Lotta handles questions on the investment framework from memory. The meeting continues. Never let a technical failure end the meeting early.

Reframe, do not apologise If a demo element fails, Christoph or Brian reframes immediately: "This is exactly why early conversations like this one matter — the things we learn from you in these sessions are what makes the system robust. You are seeing it at an honest moment." This is true, and it is the right thing to say.

Rehearsal requirement

Minimum two full rehearsals of the Phase 04 sequence before the first May meeting. Rehearsal must include: full Vineyard brief (prospective flow), CoreLens with a live participant, and at least one deliberate failure drill (Dirk pulls the plug; team executes fallback). Schedule by May 5.
