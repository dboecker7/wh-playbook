Framing

Two audiences. One meeting.
Both must leave believing.

Each May meeting contains two distinct audiences who need fundamentally different things from the same 100 minutes. The playbook must address both simultaneously without the meeting feeling split or unfocused. TARA is the red thread that holds the two narratives together.

Investor audience
  • Evaluating the platform's commercial viability and defensibility
  • Key selling points:
    • Institutional-grade investment framework (Singer Moberg 4-horizon, peer-reviewed) — previously available only to large institutions
    • Renato Staub's Guardian Intelligence: the only forward-looking geometric risk model built for family wealth — validated by 500 independent 30-year Monte Carlo simulations
    • Proprietary covariance matrix (7–8 layers) covering all asset classes — cannot be replicated by standard advisors
    • AI + deep investment expertise: the only combination at this quality level in the market
    • CaaS architecture: WH is instance one of a repeatable white-label platform — scalable without proportional headcount growth
    • Family offices are shifting from short-term to long-horizon strategy — exactly where WH plays
  • Key question: "Is this a sustainable business that outperforms the alternatives available to us?"
  • Success signal: Schedules a due diligence session with Christoph + Alex
Family office / client audience
  • Evaluating whether TARA is genuinely useful for their family's most important decisions
  • Key selling points:
    • Singer Moberg framework: portfolio structured across 4 nested horizons (Policy/Strategic/Dynamic/Tactical) — each with its own allocation logic and risk model, personalised to your family's situation
    • Renato's Guardian Intelligence: forward-looking risk signals that see the hidden beta drift and structural risks your current advisors are missing — surfaced by TARA before your quarterly meeting
    • PAR (Proportional Allocation Rule): your portfolio automatically positioned relative to valuation gaps — discipline your advisors cannot sustain under market pressure
    • TARA as companion: she watches your family between meetings, notices patterns before they become crises, and structures decisions before you have to ask
    • Fully personalised: CoreLens adapts every TARA interaction to how each family member actually thinks
  • Key question: "Will this actually help my family make better decisions at the moments that matter?"
  • Success signal: Requests a NorthStar session / onboarding conversation
The dual narrative principle Every element of the meeting speaks to both audiences at once. The SM/Renato framework is the "ticket to the concert" for investors (institutional grade) AND a demonstration of personalised clarity for clients (my portfolio, forward-looking, across four horizons). TARA is architectural defensibility for investors AND companion intelligence for families.
Which audience is in the room? Before each meeting, Christoph must confirm: investor-primary, client-primary, or both? The phase notes flag where to expand or compress accordingly.

Renato Staub — Guardian Intelligence

Two tracks — choose per meeting

Track A — Lotta presents (plain language, all meetings)

Core message: "Traditional risk models are backward-looking. When volatility spikes — exactly when you need your risk model most — they systematically understate your actual exposure. The Guardian Intelligence Engine fixes this."

Three points to cover:

  • Risk compounds, it doesn't add. In a high-stress regime, what looks like a portfolio with β = 0.50 is actually carrying β = 0.91 in realized risk. Traditional portfolios carry 82% more market risk than measured. (Show the Hidden Beta Drift chart.)
  • PAR: proportional allocation rule. We size positions proportional to the valuation gap — when markets are cheap, we increase exposure; when expensive, we reduce. Mathematical discipline. No emotion. (Show the PAR convexity gain chart.)
  • Forward-looking, not backward. 500 independent 30-year simulations validate this. PAR delivers structural convexity and drawdown protection — not outperformance promises.

Simulator — shown in every meeting

Guardian Intelligence simulator (singermobergstaub.manus.space) Show the Value-Price Gap simulation and Monte Carlo validation live in every meeting. Adjust one or two parameters (mean reversion speed, PAR scaling factor) to show it responding. Keep it to 3 minutes. Offer the link to interested participants as a self-service exploration tool — a "light version" they can use independently after the meeting.

Track B — Renato presents (quant-specialist audiences, optional)

Renato covers the mathematical framework in depth: geometric risk aggregation formula, PAR derivation, multilayer covariance architecture (7–8 layers), horizon-specific matrices. Fields technical questions on the model. Duration: up to 15 minutes if the room warrants it — compress Phase 03 accordingly.

Preparation

Objection catalogue

"Why do I need AI for this? My current advisors know my family well."
Your advisors know your family within the constraints of their attention — quarterly meetings, a few hours a year. They cannot simultaneously monitor your children's conversations, your liquidity trajectory across four time horizons, and pattern drift in how you talk about your operating company — and notice when those converge into a decision forming without you realising it. TARA can. She does not replace your advisors. She makes sure nothing falls between the meetings.
Best answered by: Brian Singer
"What happens to my family's data?"
Your personal data lives in your PersonalSphere — cryptographically isolated from WH's systems and every other family. WH advisors see structured analytical summaries that TARA generates — never your private conversations or raw data. The architecture enforces this at the API level; it is a technical constraint, not just a policy.
Best answered by: Christoph Zahneissen / Alex Papratilov
"Is this regulated? What are the compliance implications?"
TARA does not give financial advice — she gives decision intelligence and surfaces structured options for human advisors and family decision makers. The structured brief explicitly directs financial decisions to the WH advisor. Data residency, audit logs, consent management, and provenance tracking are architectural requirements. Specific regulatory questions by jurisdiction — we address those in a technical session.
Best answered by: Christoph Zahneissen / Brian Singer
"This is still prototype-grade. Why are you showing it now?"
Because the families and investors who will shape what this becomes need to be part of defining it — not inheriting a finished product. What you saw today is real: CoreLens works, the conversation pipeline works, the decision brief is structurally correct. The architecture is solid. We are showing it now because the right anchor relationships are built on shared understanding, not polished brochures.
Best answered by: Brian Singer
"I already have excellent advisors. Where does TARA fit?"
TARA works alongside your existing advisors. She surfaces what they might miss between conversations: the pattern in how your son talks about liquidity, the governance tension emerging before the board meeting, the NorthStar conflict in a scenario that looks financially optimal. She brings your advisors' attention to the right things at the right time. Think of her as the intelligence layer underneath the relationship — not a competing relationship.
Best answered by: Lotta Moberg
[Investor] "What is the path to commercial scale?"
WH is instance one of a CaaS (Companion as a Service) architecture. The platform is built to be white-labelled and deployed for other advisory contexts. Revenue combines platform fees from the CaaS layer with advisory relationship fees from WH's direct client relationships. The anchor client and investor relationships from May are the foundation of both revenue streams.
Best answered by: Christoph Zahneissen
Contingency

Failure protocol

Decision owner

Christoph Zahneissen is the demo operator and failure decision maker. If something breaks, Christoph decides immediately: retry, fallback, or skip. Nobody else intervenes.

CoreLens fails (4a)

Switch immediately to preloaded profiles. "Let me show you two profiles we prepared in advance." No explanation needed.

TARA portfolio conversation fails (4b)

Switch to recorded conversation backup. Brian narrates: "Let me walk you through the kind of conversation TARA has about a portfolio." Convert to narrative demonstration.

Vineyard brief fails (4c)

Switch to reactive flow script: "Thomas has just called to say he wants to sell the Vineyard." Brief output is identical; only §1 differs. Rehearsed and ready.

Total TARA failure

Switch to pre-loaded static walkthrough of the Vineyard brief (PDF or slides showing the six-section output). Christoph or Brian narrates. Lotta handles investment framework questions. Never let a technical failure end the meeting early.

Reframe, do not apologise If a demo element fails, Brian reframes immediately: "This is exactly why early conversations like this matter — what we learn from you makes the system robust. You are seeing it at an honest moment." This is true, and it is the right thing to say.

Rehearsal requirement

Minimum two full rehearsals of Phase 04 before the first May meeting, including at least one deliberate failure drill. Recommended: May 5–7. Christoph owns scheduling.
